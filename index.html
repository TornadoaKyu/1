The POMODORO Technique

Warning: This article does not describe the author and is based solely on research. But, of course, it will be an educational and inspiring research for the author.

We can use the pomodoro technique if we can't focus on studying for long periods of time. Basically, we can either use the 25 minute study time, and 5 minute break or (if we have endless potential right now) 50 minute study time and 10 minute break would do. Don't you worry! every 4 turns, we'll get a long deserved 15/20 minute break.

It helps us gain back control over our work day by encouraging moments of complete focus. It helps us get rid of the procrastination stress by motivating us to get stuff done, one Pomodoro at a time. Frequent breaks help keep our mind focused on the task at hand.

Speaking of Procrastination:

"The activity of putting off tasks we don't enjoy, despite the consequences"

Procrastination is the act of delaying or putting off tasks until the last minute, or past their deadline. Some researchers define procrastination as a "form of self-regulation failure characterized by the irrational delay of tasks despite potentially negative consequences.(What Is Procrastination?, 2022)

These are some methods we can also use for studying:

Both in planning and retaining information for our exams:

To-do lists

By keeping such a list, we make sure that our tasks are written down all in one place so we don't forget anything important. (MindTools | Home, n.d.)

Blurting

Write down as much information as we can remember on the chosen subject area, once we have written everything we can remember, then research the areas we missed and write those areas down to revise further.(Six Best Revision Techniques, n.d.)

Eat/bath/sleep/revise/repeat

When we are revising, short study sessions followed by short naps are considered one of the best ways to learn. You read that right – it’s actually beneficial to take naps.(Six Best Revision Techniques, n.d.)

Stay Curious

“The important thing is to not stop questioning. Curiosity has its own reason for existing.” -Albert Einstein

Review Notes From Class Every Evening

Review and elaborate on the class notes after school. Reviewing your notes will assist you the next time you have a difficult test by transferring information from short-term memory to long-term memory.

Simplify Study Notes

Make studying less overwhelming by condensing notes from class. Underline or highlight key words. Create visual aids like charts, story webs, mind maps, or outlines to organize and simplify information and help you remember better.
